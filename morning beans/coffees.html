Light Roast

Light: Cinnamon, American, half city, moderate-light roast[23] After several minutes the beans pop or crack and visibly expand in size. This stage is called first crack. Dry Lighter-bodied, higher acidity, no obvious roast flavor. This level of roast is ideal for tasting the full origin character of the coffee.

Medium Roast

City, city+, full city: After being developed through first crack, the coffee reaches these roast levels. Dry Sugars have been further caramelized, and acidity has been muted. This results in coffee with higher body, but some roast flavor imposed.

Dark Roast

Full city+, Italian, Viennese, French After a few more minutes the beans begin popping again, and oils rise to the surface. This is called second crack. Shiny. The level of oil correlates to how far the coffee is taken past second crack. Bittersweet flavors are prominent, aromas and flavors of roast become clearly evident. Little, if any, origin character remains.
